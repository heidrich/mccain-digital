01 — What we build

From idea to App Store.

Four things, done properly — one shared codebase that feels native on both platforms, talks to a real backend, and survives store review and the years of updates after it.

One codebase, both stores

React Native and Expo, written once and shipped to iOS and Android — with real native modules (Swift, Kotlin) dropped in wherever a screen needs to be fully native. One team, one code base, not two builds drifting apart over time.

Native feel, not a webview

Real native views: fluid gestures, offline-first storage, push notifications, deep links and 60fps animation via Reanimated. It scrolls, swipes and responds the way a native app should — because under the hood it is one.

Store-ready, end to end

App Store and Play Store review, signing, release and the review back-and-forth — handled. EAS builds and over-the-air updates ship fixes to users in hours, not a week-long re-review, so the app keeps improving after launch.

Backed by a real backend

Auth, sync, APIs and storage — the same modern stack we put behind a web app. Supabase or Firebase, REST or GraphQL, your data layer built properly so the app isn't a pretty shell over nothing.

04 — How it goes

In your hands early.

You won't wait months to see your app. We get a real build onto your phone via TestFlight and internal tracks within weeks — then iterate on something you can actually hold.

A working session on what the app must do on day one — the one flow that matters — and what waits for later. You get a build plan and a fixed quote, not a feature wishlist.

02

The key screens, real navigation, real gestures — running on a device. We get the native feel right early, because that's the part a mockup can never tell you.

The full app, wired to its backend, shipped to you continuously via TestFlight and Play internal tracks. You use each build, we fix what's off — no big-bang reveal at the end.
